Why I Started Coding

When people ask why I started coding, I often find myself reflecting on the journey that led me to this exciting new world.
It is a combination of curiosity, ambition, and the belief that it is never too late to learn something new.

For as long as I can remember, I have always been curious about how things work, whether it was the mechanics of a machine, the artistry of a design, or, in this case, the technology that powers our modern lives.
I grew up surrounded by the beauty of Bali, a place rich in creativity and tradition, and that sense of exploration stayed with me as I transitioned to a new chapter of my life in Munich, Germany.

When I moved to Munich in 2023, I wanted to challenge myself in a way I never had before. Coding felt like the perfect challenge skill that combines problem-solving, creativity, and the potential to make a real impact.
It is a language of the future, one that connects people across the globe, and I wanted to be a part of it.

Another reason I started coding was the opportunity it offers to continuously learn and grow. Technology evolves so quickly, and with it comes the chance to stay curious and adapt.
As someone who has always been passionate about learning, coding feels like an adventure that never ends.

But perhaps the most personal reason I started coding is my dream of working in the IT industry. I want to build solutions that matter, whether that is through websites, applications, or something I can not even imagine yet.
The thought of creating something from scratch, of turning an idea into reality with just a keyboard and some knowledge, is both empowering and exciting.

Of course, learning to code is not without its challenges. I have had moments of doubt, frustration, and even confusion. But those moments are outweighed by the thrill of getting something to work for the first time, no matter how small.
It is those tiny victories that remind me why I started and keep me moving forward.

Through this blog, I hope to share my experiences, from the challenges I face to the joy of overcoming them.
I also hope it inspires others to start their own journeys, whether in coding or in something else they have always dreamed of.
